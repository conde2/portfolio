Hi SneakyBox team. I grew up loving MMOs, and somewhere along the way I stopped just playing them and started building them. Networking is the part I love the most: that small moment of magic when what one player does shows up on everyone else's screen, fast and exactly right. When it works, no one notices. That is the part I care about getting right.

I have worked on real MMOs that people actually play, like Ravendawn, Grandline Adventures and Drakantos. Most of that work was deep in the netcode: server and client talking over TCP, written in C++ and Lua, with all the real trade-offs of latency, bandwidth and keeping the game world in sync. Those projects taught me how multiplayer behaves when it leaves a clean test setup and meets thousands of players.

Right now I am building my own game in C# and Unity, using PurrNet for the networking, and I am loving it. It keeps me close to the same questions you face every day, just from a different angle. I would love to bring that to SneakyBox, learn your platforms and services, and help make games that feel smooth no matter where someone is playing. Networking is what I would happily do for the rest of my career, and your team feels like the right place to do it.
